About Our Project

Our data provides a visualization of homelessness in the United States over the last thirteen years (2007 – 2019) for each state. Data was extracted from the Housing Urban Development (“HUD”) exchange which shows changes in homelessness percentage for each state (2007 – 2019), total beds available (Housing Inventory Count) and census population information.

The dashboard reveals states with the largest percentage increase and decrease in homelessness, the states with the largest number of total beds and the states with the least number of total beds. The purpose for carrying out this analytical review was to determine whether states with the largest increase in homelessness also had the largest number of beds to accommodate the increase in homelessness.

We also analyzed Point-In-Time (PIT) data information available through HUD to determine demographic categories of the homeless population (2007 – 2012).
